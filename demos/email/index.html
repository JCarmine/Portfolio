  2. Overview

    Email coding requires the use of coding techniques that were used in 1999 (literally). The code is simple, redundant, and devoid of any of the newer web technologies. Instead, a successful email relies on the use of tables, images, inline CSS, and a handful of coding tricks to make it display correctly in every inbox.

    In fact, it’s extremely difficult to build an email that looks great across the board. An in-depth knowledge of the display issues present in various email clients, and the coding tricks required to address them, is required to develop a successful campaign.

  3. The Header

    Any good newsletter will benefit from having a large header that includes stunning imagery to capture the reader’s attention. It also serves as a great location to include your organization’s logo, and provide a Call to Action (CTA).

    Unfortunately, it isn’t possible to use background images in email, and lay rendered “live” text over them. Instead, I will develop the entire section in Adobe Photoshop, and include it as an image.

  4. Fonts and Text

    The main message of your email should always be displayed using “live” text that is written into the HTML, and laid over a solid background of any color (also written into the code). This method is favored over just loading another image as a majority of email clients are set to automatically block the images in your email, and this could prevent your message from being seen.

    In addition, HTML emails are limited to the use of a handful of “web safe” fonts to ensure that they display consistently across all clients. But rest assured, your email will be designed with this in mind and the fonts worked seamlessly into the design.

  6. Link to Your Site

    An invitation or link for the reader to visit your site can also be placed in the email. Links should contain special tracking information that will allow you to tell how many people are visiting your site because of the email. This tracking information is critical for measuring the success of your email campaign.

  7. Social Media

    Email newsletters are also a great way to drive traffic to your social media presence.
